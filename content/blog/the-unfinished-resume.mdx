You may know by the heading what I'm about to write — it's my version of *jack of all trades, master of none*. Why mastering one?

I failed at JEE. I failed at being the topper of my class. I failed at sports. I failed at trading. I failed at coding.

Not dramatically. Not with a story worth telling at a dinner table.

## How it started

I was in 12th standard in 2021, amid COVID. I was talking to my girlfriend — we were in the middle of a fight — and then she said, *"I don't want to be with someone who doesn't have any goal in life, like what he wants to do after school."*

I felt so bad. Why don't I have any goal? What do I want to achieve?

After that day I started searching for what I wanted to become, what I should learn and study, and how to set goals in life. As a nerd, I googled *"tell me some careers/streams one should pursue after 12th"* and got suggestions: doctor (but I'm from the Maths science stream), CA, BA, BALLB — and Engineering, which caught my eye.

I read about big tech leaders: Mark Zuckerberg, Bill Gates, Elon Musk, Satya Nadella, and Sundar Pichai. I got fascinated — one thing about me is I get motivated too easily.

## JEE and the drop year

I searched for the best colleges in India for Engineering and found that IITs are the top colleges for B.Tech. And how do you get into them? By fighting with JEE.

I was still in 12th but also preparing for JEE. I filled the exam form, gave my first JEE Mains exam — it was pretty nice and I got 66 percentile. I was happy; that was good for a first attempt.

In 2021, JEE was held four times. Here comes the interesting part: my percentile degraded with every attempt, and I got 30 percentile in my last (4th) attempt. I gave MHT-CET too — not good marks there either.

After 12th I took a drop year for preparation (2021–2022), took a course from Physics Wallah, and thought I'd crack the exam. I appeared for a 5th attempt — nice, but my percentile was lower than the previous year. My last attempt still scored low. I was also preparing for MHT-CET.

## Trading

Amid all this I got introduced to trading. Crypto trading became one of the best moments of my life — I learned how to trade cryptocurrency, coins, meme-coins. I invested time learning charts and patterns. I was pretty good at trading sometimes, making good profits, but big losses covered them so I often closed at break even.

I started trading a lot — 10–12 trades a day, small profits, small losses, sometimes getting liquidated. I got anxiety. I was enjoying trading (I still do), but I ran out of funds and stopped. Whenever I have capital, I still trade.

I also learned stock market basics and option trading. The market only rewards patience — *in the stock market, money flows from the impatient to the patient*. I didn't have patience at that time. I'm still learning, and you have to pay a fee to the market for learning — I'm talking about losses.

## College

Last months of 2022 — JEE last attempt results (didn't make it). MHT-CET result was good, but your MHT-CET marks don't count if you're outside Maharashtra. Everything felt shattered. I cried in front of my parents for the first time — shivering, fever, everything. But my parents were always there. They said don't worry, there is always a way. I calmed down.

This is the beginning of a new story. I took admission in a tier 3 college in a tier 3 city. First day of college I was already late for my first class. People were talking about the fresher's party the next day, and I had to shift to a hostel that same day — so I didn't go.

## Coding

2023 — I made some friends at college and hostel. I started learning how to code. Started with C, did some small projects. Then I saw a reel on Instagram about a full-stack developer course — a 5-day bootcamp for ₹100. I enrolled.

The bootcamp started. I learned HTML and CSS. I was so happy building my own website — that was fascinating. I enjoyed learning and building projects. Then the tutor said take the full course for more money. I purchased without thinking (borrowed ₹6K from my sister for 3 months).

I was learning web development — HTML, CSS, JavaScript, frameworks, tools — making small projects. After about a month, the tutor scammed the whole batch. There were only incomplete recordings left. I learned something in web development, but I always think: if I had continued, maybe I'd be working somewhere or have a good portfolio. I didn't. I switched to something new. Switching became my hobby. I wanted to learn everything at the same time — and ended up doing nothing, only thinking.

Then I researched YouTube channels to learn React, completed a whole playlist over months (not consistently), built small projects — one of them is a blogging website still incomplete till date.

## DSA and AI

I heard that for getting a job in big tech (FAANG) you have to learn DSA. I started DSA — arrays, stopped for a while, started arrays again. It was a never-ending loop. Some LeetCode questions, becoming consistent, then stopping again. Then starting once more — now I'm at linked lists. DSA feels like something I'll never complete, even after death.

Then I got to know about Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning. I started learning Python, data analysis, data visualisation, and machine learning. Still learning.

## X (Twitter)

In 2025 I started using Twitter (now X) again. I had an account from 2021 when I was trading crypto. I started my main account **imPU23** — the username inspired by Virat Kohli's **imVkohli**. I started spending time on X, reading tweets, and learned you can get paid for tweeting.

I purchased X Premium on 31st January. I got verified — a blue tick. I was happy because this time I wasn't only thinking, I executed something. I started learning how to build your brand online, getting reach, staying updated on AI news, trying new posting formats.

My account was growing — first 100 followers made me so happy. Hitting 100k–450k impressions in a day. I was learning patience for the first time in my life.

Then one day I got a notification — an email saying my X account was suspended for violating rules. I submitted an appeal and waited. Nothing happened for a week. I started once again from zero with a new account: **imdevPU23**. In two days: 80 followers, 20k impressions, hosted a Space with 100+ people joining. I was happy.

## The point

I was thinking of writing this article for a long time, and now I am.

My whole point is: start learning new things, start executing things you think about — it feels so nice when you're doing the thing you once only thought about.

It's okay to master none. No one is the master of any skill — they are still learning and figuring out. But don't only think about starting new things. **Just do it.**

This is my story of being that Jack. The failures, the random wins, and the part where I'm still figuring it out.

Thanks for reading — this was my first writing.

~ Priyanshu
